Support Call Escalation Procedure

  1. Front-Line Support:

    Criteria for Escalation:

    • Front-line support should attempt to resolve common and known issues using standard procedures.
    • Escalation may be necessary when an issue falls outside the scope of front-line support or requires specialized knowledge or resources.

    Examples of Escalation Criteria:

    • Technical issue is complex and not covered by the standard troubleshooting guide.
    • Customer reports a critical system outage affecting multiple users.
    • Support agent lacks the necessary expertise or access to resolve the problem.
    • Issue requires further investigation or diagnostics beyond front-line capabilities.

  2. Second-Level Support:

    Criteria for Escalation:

    • Second-level support is responsible for handling more complex technical issues that require in-depth expertise or access to specialized tools and resources.
    • Escalation to third-level support should occur when the issue remains unresolved at the second level or requires even more specialized knowledge or resources.

    Examples of Escalation Criteria:

    • The issue involves complex software bugs that require development expertise.
    • Extensive troubleshooting has been performed at the second level, but the problem persists.
    • Escalation to third-level support may be triggered by issues related to critical infrastructure components.

  3. Third-Level Support:

    Criteria for Escalation:

    • Third-level support is the highest level of technical expertise within the support organization. Calls are escalated to this level when the issue requires highly specialized knowledge or resources.
    • Escalation to third-level support is typically reserved for the most complex and critical problems.

    Examples of Escalation Criteria:

    • The issue involves rare and highly technical problems that require expert-level knowledge.
    • Extensive troubleshooting at the second level and collaboration with third-party vendors have been exhausted.
    • The issue involves security breaches or major data loss that requires immediate attention.

Support Call Escalation Process

  1. Front-Line Support:

    Escalation Process: When an issue meets the escalation criteria, it should be escalated to the second-level support team. Front-line support agents provide basic troubleshooting and assistance.

  2. Second-Level Support:

    Escalation Process: Second-level support experts perform in-depth troubleshooting. When an issue meets the criteria for second-level support, it should be escalated to the third-level support team.

  3. Third-Level Support:

    Escalation Process: When issues are extremely complex, critical, or involve highly specialized knowledge. Resolution of the most challenging and specialized issues occurs at this level.

Troubleshooting

  1. Can you describe the problem in detail?
  2. Have you tried restarting the device?
  3. Is the issue occurring consistently, or is it intermittent?
  4. Did this issue start after any recent updates or changes?
  5. Have you checked for software updates or patches?
  6. Is the problem specific to certain applications or tasks?
  7. Are there any unusual noises or hardware issues?
  8. Have you tried using the device on another network?
  9. Can you provide the model and version information of your device?
  10. Have you recently installed any new software or hardware?
  11. Have you cleared cache and cookies (if applicable)?
  12. Have you tested the issue on a different user account (if applicable)?
  13. Is there anything else you think might be relevant?
